# FamCloud LLC Setup Checklist

## Step 1: Choose the Name
- [ ] Check name availability in your state (search [state].gov/business-search)
- [ ] Reserve the name if required (some states allow reservation before filing)
- **Name options:** FamCloud LLC, Hearth AI LLC, FamCloud Technologies LLC
- **Tip:** The LLC name doesn't have to match the product name. You can do business as "FamCloud" under any LLC.

## Step 2: Choose the State
- [ ] **Where you're registered** (your home state) — simplest, cheapest, but state income tax applies
- [ ] **Delaware** — standard for startups, $310/year franchise tax, but you'll also pay your home state's fees
- [ ] **Wyoming** — cheapest ($100/year), no state income tax, but you still need to register in your home state as a foreign LLC if you live there
- **Recommendation for now:** Your home state. Simplest, cheapest, and you're not raising venture capital yet.

## Step 3: Register the LLC
- [ ] File Articles of Organization with your state's Secretary of State
- [ ] Cost: $50-$500 depending on state
- [ ] Can be done online in 15-30 minutes
- [ ] **Services:** ZenBusiness, LegalZoom, or DIY through your state's website

## Step 4: Get an EIN (Employer Identification Number)
- [ ] Free from the IRS: https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online
- [ ] Takes 10 minutes, instant confirmation
- [ ] You'll need this for banking, taxes, and hiring

## Step 5: Open a Business Bank Account
- [ ] Bring your EIN confirmation, LLC Articles, and personal ID
- [ ] Options: Mercury (online, free, startup-friendly), Chase, Wells Fargo
- [ ] **Separate all business expenses from personal** — this is critical for liability protection

## Step 6: Operating Agreement
- [ ] Even for a single-member LLC, write one
- [ ] It says: who owns it, how decisions get made, what happens if things go wrong
- [ ] Template: https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/f4562.pdf or search "single member LLC operating agreement template"
- [ ] Not legally required in most states, but banks and courts expect it

## Step 7: Business Licenses & Permits
- [ ] Check your city/county for local business license requirements
- [ ] Most areas don't require a special license for software/AI services
- [ ] If you're going to people's homes to install equipment, check if you need a contractor's license

## Step 8: Taxes
- [ ] Single-member LLC = "disregarded entity" by default — income flows to your personal tax return
- [ ] Consider filing as an S-Corp later if revenue gets significant (saves on self-employment tax)
- [ ] Quarterly estimated taxes (April, June, September, January)
- [ ] Track expenses: hardware, software, travel, home office, internet

## Step 9: Insurance
- [ ] General liability insurance (~$500-1,000/year) — protects if something breaks at a customer's house
- [ ] E&O (Errors & Omissions) insurance — protects if your AI gives bad advice
- [ ] **Not needed on day one** — get it when you have your first paying customer

## Step 10: Domain + Branding
- [ ] Register famcloud.ai (or whichever name you choose)
- [ ] Set up a simple landing page (even just "coming soon")
- [ ] Get a business email: charles@famcloud.ai

---

## Timeline: Can be done in 1-2 evenings
- Step 1-2 (name + state): 30 minutes
- Step 3 (file LLC): 30 minutes
- Step 4 (EIN): 10 minutes
- Step 5 (bank account): 1-2 hours (appointments may be needed)
- Step 6 (operating agreement): 30 minutes
- Step 7 (licenses): 30 minutes (research only)
- Steps 8-10: Do as needed, not all at once

## Total Cost: $100-$500
- State filing fee: $50-$500
- EIN: Free
- Bank account: Free (Mercury) or $0-$25/month
- Insurance: Later, when you have customers

---

**You can do all of this while employed.** The LLC is just a legal wrapper — it doesn't affect your day job.
