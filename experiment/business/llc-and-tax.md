# FamCloud — Business Structure, LLC, Tax & Growth Paths

## Business Structure Timeline

| When | Structure | Why |
|------|-----------|-----|
| Now (0 customers) | Sole Proprietorship | Simplest, just file Schedule C |
| 10+ customers ($5k+/mo) | LLC | Asset protection (~$100-200 to file) |
| $30k+/year profit | LLC + S-Corp election | Saves 7-10% on taxes via salary/distributions split |

### Key Clarification: LLC and S-Corp aren't mutually exclusive

An LLC is a **legal entity**. S-Corp is a **tax status**. You can be an LLC that elects S-Corp taxation.

**LLC + S-Corp in practice:** Pay yourself a reasonable salary ($40k-60k W-2), take the rest as distributions (not subject to 15.3% self-employment tax). At $500k profit the savings are ~$67k/year.

### Tax Deductions Available
Hardware, home office, car mileage ($0.67/mile), phone, internet, domain/hosting, software subscriptions, health insurance, retirement (SEP-IRA/Solo 401k up to $66k-69k), Section 179 depreciation, travel, education.

## Growth Paths

### Path A: Lifestyle (Recommended for Charles)
One person, no employees, no outside funding. At 500 customers: ~$285k/year. Total control, can be semi-passive if automated. Timeline: 2-4 years.

### Path B: Bootstrap
Reinvest profits, hire 1-2 people, 1000-5000 customers over 3-5 years. Still own 100%, build something sellable. More complexity but real company trajectory.

### Path C: Venture-Backed (Not Recommended)
VCs need $100M+ potential. FamCloud at $28 × 10k customers = $2.8M/year — too small for VC. Massive pressure, dilution to 10-25%, lose control.

### Bootstrap at 2,000 Customers
Revenue: ~$1,344,000/year. Year 2+ take-home: ~$730k-$770k. Acquisition potential: $4-7M (MicroAcquire) to $10M+ (strategic). But the better play is keeping it — $730k+/year cash flow for as long as customers stay.

## Setup Checklist

1. Check LLC name availability in your state
2. File Articles of Organization ($50-500 depending on state)
3. Get EIN from IRS (free, 10 min, instant)
4. Open business bank account (Mercury, free, startup-friendly)
5. Write single-member LLC operating agreement (template online)
6. Check local business license requirements
7. When profitable >$30k: file Form 2553 for S-Corp election
