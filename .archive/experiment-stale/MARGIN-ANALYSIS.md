## Margins in Plain English

**Year 1:** Hardware cost eats margins. Basic barely breaks even (1%). Pro does okay (39%). Premium is strong (61%). Lease is decent (42%).

**Year 2+:** Hardware is paid off. **90%+ margins across the board.** This is where the business prints money.

**At scale (100+ customers, Year 2+):**
- You're collecting $30-75/month per customer
- Each costs $4.60/month to serve
- **You keep $25.40-$70.40 of every dollar**

## The Margin Secret

**Year 1 margins are low because of hardware. Year 2+ margins are ~91% because hardware is paid off and the customer keeps paying.**

This is the same model as:
- **Adobe Creative Cloud:** $600/year, 95%+ margins (software, no hardware)
- **SaaS companies:** 80-95% margins (no COGS after development)
- **Streaming services:** 70-85% margins (content costs are fixed)

**FamCloud is a SaaS model with hardware as a one-time customer acquisition cost.** Once the hardware is paid off (5-11 months depending on tier), it's a 90%+ margin recurring revenue business.

The key insight: **hardware is not a recurring cost, it's a customer acquisition cost.** You buy it once per customer, they pay $30-75/month for years. After month 5-11, every dollar is almost pure profit.
