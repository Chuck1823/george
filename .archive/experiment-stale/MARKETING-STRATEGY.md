## Referral Program

**Mechanism:** "Give $50, Get $50"
- Existing customer refers a friend
- Friend gets $50 off their setup fee
- Referrer gets $50 credit (applied to next 2 months)
- Both parties happy, customer acquisition cost = $50 credit

**Implementation:**
- Each customer gets a unique referral code
- Friend enters code during signup
- System tracks referrals automatically
- Credit applied to billing automatically

**Expected impact:** 20-30% of new customers come from referrals (industry average for referral programs)

## The Marketing Problem We Still Need to Solve

**The hardest part:** Explaining a new product category in one sentence. FamCloud isn't "just another AI assistant." It's a fundamentally different thing: home-based, cross-platform, memory-heavy, privacy-first, family-focused. That's hard to communicate quickly.

**What we need to solve:**
- One-sentence pitch that makes people GET it immediately
- Landing page that converts at 5-10% (industry average for SaaS)
- Ad creative that stops people scrolling
- Trust signals (early adopters will want proof this works)
- Social proof (how do you get reviews without customers?)

**What makes this easier:**
- The product is genuinely useful (if we build it right)
- The value prop is clear (family assistant + memory + privacy)
- The pricing is reasonable ($150 + $50/month)
- The category is new (no direct competitors to explain against)

**What makes this hard:**
- Nobody knows what FamCloud is (awareness problem)
- $150 upfront + $50/month is a commitment (trust problem)
- "Local AI" sounds technical (complexity problem)
- Setup sounds hard (friction problem) — even though it's just "plug in + scan QR"
