# LLC vs S-Corp + Bootstrap at 2,000 Customers

## LLC vs S-Corp: The Actual Difference

**Important clarification:** LLC and S-Corp aren't mutually exclusive. An LLC is a legal entity. S-Corp is a TAX STATUS. You can be an **LLC that elects S-Corp taxation**.

### LLC (Default = Pass-Through Taxation)
- **What it is:** Legal entity that protects personal assets
- **Tax treatment:** All profit flows to your personal tax return (Schedule C)
- **Self-employment tax:** 15.3% on ALL profit
- **Pros:** Simple, cheap to set up ($50-150), asset protection, flexible
- **Cons:** Pay 15.3% self-employment tax on everything
- **Best when:** Profit < $30k/year

### LLC + S-Corp Election
- **What it is:** LLC entity + IRS S-Corp tax election (Form 2553)
- **Tax treatment:** Split income into SALARY (W-2) + DISTRIBUTIONS
- **How it saves tax:**
  - You pay yourself a "reasonable salary" (say $60k/year)
  - Salary is subject to FICA (15.3% = Social Security + Medicare)
  - REST of profit is DISTRIBUTIONS — NOT subject to self-employment tax
  - You save 15.3% tax on the distribution portion
- **Pros:** Saves 7-10% on taxes vs LLC alone. Still asset protection. Still simple.
- **Cons:** Need to run payroll (extra paperwork/cost ~$500-1,000/year for payroll service). Must pay reasonable salary (can't pay $0 salary and take all as distributions to avoid tax).
- **Best when:** Profit > $30k/year

### S-Corp (Direct, Not LLC)
- **What it is:** Corporation entity with S-Corp tax election
- **Same tax treatment** as LLC + S-Corp election
- **More formalities:** Board meetings, bylaws, more paperwork
- **Best when:** You want corporate structure for investor/liability reasons
- **Charles doesn't need this.** LLC + S-Corp election is simpler and same tax benefit.

### Summary Table

| Feature | LLC (Pass-Through) | LLC + S-Corp |
|---------|--------------------|--------------|
| Asset Protection | ✅ | ✅ |
| Setup Cost | $50-150 | $50-150 + $100 (S-Corp election) |
| Annual Complexity | Low | Medium (payroll required) |
| Self-Employment Tax | 15.3% on ALL profit | 15.3% on salary only |
| Tax Savings at $100k profit | None | ~$6,120 saved (15.3% × $40k distributions) |
| Tax Savings at $200k profit | None | ~$21,420 saved (15.3% × $140k distributions) |
| Tax Savings at $500k profit | None | ~$67,320 saved (15.3% × $440k distributions) |

### Recommendation for Charles:
- **Now (0 customers):** Sole proprietorship (simplest)
- **At 10 customers ($5k+/mo):** Form LLC ($100-200, asset protection)
- **At $30k+/year profit:** Add S-Corp election (Form 2553, start payroll)
- **At 100+ customers:** You should definitely be an LLC + S-Corp

## Key Insight: This Is a Cash Flow Business

The real value of FamCloud isn't the exit — it's the **cash flow**. 

At 2,000 customers, you're pulling $730k-$770k per year after all costs and taxes. Over 10 years, that's **$7.3M-$7.7M of take-home income** without ever selling.

If you sell at $5M-$10M, you get a lump sum. If you keep it, you get $730k-$770k every single year for as long as customers stay.

**Which is better?** It depends. A $5M exit is a single event. $730k/year for 10+ years is ongoing wealth. If the business stays growing, keeping it is probably better financially.
